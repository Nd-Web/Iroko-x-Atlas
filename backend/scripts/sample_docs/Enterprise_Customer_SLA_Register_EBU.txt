2. SLA TIER DEFINITIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1  Tier 1 — Enterprise Premium

     Contracted Uptime    : 99.9% per calendar month
     Measurement Window  : 24 × 7, including planned maintenance windows
                           (must be pre-notified 72 hours in advance)
     SLA Credit Formula  : 10% of monthly contract value per hour of
                           downtime below contracted uptime
     Maximum Credit      : 50% of monthly contract value in any month
     Escalation          : Dedicated Account Manager + VP Enterprise response
                           within 2 hours of P1 fault notification

2.2  Tier 2 — Enterprise Standard

     Contracted Uptime   : 99.5% per calendar month
     SLA Credit Formula  : 5% of monthly contract value per hour of downtime
     Maximum Credit      : 30% of monthly contract value in any month
     Escalation          : Account Manager response within 4 hours

2.3  Tier 3 — Enterprise Basic

     Contracted Uptime   : 99.0% per calendar month
     SLA Credit Formula  : 2% of monthly contract value per hour of downtime
     Maximum Credit      : 20% of monthly contract value in any month
     Escalation          : Standard NOC process

3. SLA TERMS AND CREDIT SCHEDULE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1  SLA Credit Calculation

     The SLA credit is calculated as follows:

         Monthly Credit = (Hours of Downtime × Monthly Contract Value
                          × Credit Rate per Hour)

         Where Credit Rate per Hour:
           Tier 1 : 10% of monthly contract value per hour
           Tier 2 : 5% of monthly contract value per hour
           Tier 3 : 2% of monthly contract value per hour

     Example (Tier 1, Zenith Bank):
       Monthly contract value: NGN 7,916,667 (NGN 95M / 12 months)
       Downtime: 4.2 hours (Ikeja cluster outage, 14 Feb 2026)
       Credit: 4.2 × 10% × NGN 7,916,667 = NGN 3,325,000 ← EXCEEDS CAP
       Credit capped at 50% of monthly value: NGN 3,958,334

     NOTE: Actual credit is 4.2 × 10% = 42% of monthly value, below the
     50% cap. Credit = NGN 3,325,000. Internal calculation shows total
     NGN 2.1M across three affected customers after proportional adjustment.

3.2  SLA Credit Process
     (a) EBU is notified by NOC of P1 outage affecting enterprise customers.
     (b) EBU Account Manager notifies affected customer within 2 hours (Tier 1).
     (c) SLA credit is calculated and confirmed within 5 business days.
     (d) Credit is applied to the following month's invoice.
     (e) Customer confirms acceptance of credit in writing.
